Two years ago, building a production chatbot meant months of work. Assembling a team, architecting the system, handling edge cases, deploying to production.

Now? A competent engineer with the right tools can ship something useful in days.

The difference isn't the technology alone — it's the combination of better models, easier integration patterns, and the ability to iterate without rewriting from scratch.

But here's what surprises teams: the speed advantage doesn't just save time. It compounds. Because now your first version ships, gets real feedback, and you can improve it based on what actually matters instead of what you guessed mattered.

The chatbot that takes 6 months to build? It ships with 100 assumptions baked in. The chatbot that ships in a week? It ships with feedback-driven iteration.

In a year, the second approach has overtaken the first by not just time saved, but quality improved.

This is the real unlock of AI tooling — not faster execution of the same plan, but the ability to discover what the real problem is before you've invested months in solving the wrong thing.
