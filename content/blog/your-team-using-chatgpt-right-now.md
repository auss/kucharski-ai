Your team is using ChatGPT right now.

Do you know about it? Probably not.

Here's the uncomfortable truth: 89% of enterprise AI usage is invisible to IT leaders. That's not a typo. Nearly 9 out of 10 AI interactions in your organization are happening in the shadows.

Even worse — 38% of employees admit to sharing sensitive data with AI apps without their employer's consent. Think about that. Customer data. Financial projections. Strategic plans. All flowing into public AI models.

I've seen this firsthand. The pattern is always the same:

- Marketing uses ChatGPT for campaign copy
- Sales feeds customer emails into AI for summaries
- Finance uploads spreadsheets for analysis
- HR uses AI to draft job descriptions

Nobody asks permission. Nobody checks the policy. Because there is no policy.

The risk isn't theoretical. GDPR fines. Data leaks. Competitive intelligence exposed. Reputational damage.

But here's what drives me crazy: most IT leaders only discover Shadow AI during a compliance audit. By then, it's damage control, not prevention.

You can't stop AI adoption. But you can govern it before it governs you.

Dealing with Shadow AI in your organization? Three things to do this week:

1. Audit what tools your teams actually use
2. Identify where sensitive data might be flowing
3. Start the governance conversation with leadership

It's not about blocking AI — it's about making it safe.
